How to Play Stack Attack

Goal

Be the first to either empty your hand OR score three stacks of cards.

Setup

You start with 7 cards. The deck has number cards in 4 colors (red, blue, green, yellow, numbered 1-9) plus action cards that let you mess with your opponent.

Your turn

  1. Play one card from your hand, OR draw a card if you can't or don't want to play
  2. After playing, you can score a stack if any of your stacks has 3 or more cards
  3. End your turn

Playing number cards

When you play a number card, it goes into a stack of the same color in front of you. You can add it to an existing stack of that color, or start a new stack. Stacks belong to you — your opponent can't add to them, but they can attack them.

Action cards (coming in Phase 3)

  • STEAL — Take the top card off one of their stacks
  • SWAP — Exchange your entire hand with theirs
  • FREEZE — They skip their next turn completely
  • WILD — Play as a number of any color you choose
  • SHUFFLE — Their stacks get reorganized randomly by color
  • DRAW 3 — They must draw 3 cards before their next play
  • REVERSE — Flip any stack upside down (top becomes bottom)
  • DOUBLE — Play another card this turn

Action cards appear in your hand but don't do anything yet — clicking shows a message. They'll fully work after Phase 3.

Scoring a stack (coming in Phase 4)

When one of your stacks has 3 or more cards, you can choose to score it after playing. The stack gets discarded and you get 1 score point. You need 3 score points to win. Only one score per turn.

Winning

Win by reaching 3 scored stacks, OR by playing your last card so your hand is empty. Currently (Phase 2): empty-hand win works, deck-exhaustion loss works, 3-stacks win comes in Phase 4.

Watch out for

  • SWAP can flip a winning position fast — keep some cards in hand even when you're close to empty
  • FREEZE + DRAW 3 combo is brutal — opponent loses a turn AND draws 3 cards
  • SHUFFLE can wreck a carefully built stack — score important stacks before they get hit
  • DOUBLE lets you chain — playing a DOUBLE then another action can swing the game

Tips for new players

  • Don't hoard. If you can play, play.
  • Score a stack when you can. Holding stacks makes them targets.
  • Save action cards for when they hurt the most — STEAL is worth more late than early.
